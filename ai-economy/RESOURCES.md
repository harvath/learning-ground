# AI Economy Resources

This topic decays fast. Every entry below should be treated as "good as of the date it was checked," and this whole file should be revisited periodically rather than trusted forever.

## Knowledge — Business & economic layer

- [Stanford HAI — AI Index Report](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index) (annual, 9th edition as of 2026)
  The single broadest annual synthesis — R&D, technical performance, economy, policy, public opinion. **Use for**: resetting the yearly baseline; the best "read once a year" document in this space.
- [Epoch AI](https://epoch.ai/)
  Primary quantitative source for compute growth, training-cost trends, and capability benchmarking. **Use for**: grounding any claim about scaling, training cost, or compute growth in actual numbers instead of vibes.
- [CB Insights — State of Venture / State of AI reports](https://www.cbinsights.com/research/)
  Primary quantitative source for funding rounds, valuations, and startup mortality. **Use for**: checking whether a funding claim is real and how it compares to the broader venture market.
- [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/) (Ben Thompson)
  Slow, structural, thesis-driven analysis of tech business models. **Use for**: understanding *why* a deal or platform shift matters long-term — not for breaking news.
- [The Information](https://www.theinformation.com/)
  Subscription tech journalism specializing in deal/valuation/internal-dynamics scoops. **Use for**: "what's actually happening inside the labs" — many of the funding-round specifics in this course trace back here.
- [Import AI](https://importai.substack.com/) (Jack Clark, Anthropic co-founder)
  Policy-and-research digest from an insider's vantage point. **Use for**: the intersection of capability progress and governance framing.
- [Dwarkesh Podcast](https://www.dwarkeshpatel.com/podcast) (Dwarkesh Patel)
  Long-form interviews with lab researchers/executives, full transcripts published. **Use for**: closest thing to a primary-source deep interview with the people actually building frontier models.
- [No Priors](https://www.no-priors.com/) (Sarah Guo, Elad Gil)
  VC/startup-market lens. **Use for**: "what's investable" framing, as a counterweight to pure research depth.

## Knowledge — Technical ecosystem layer

- [Anthropic — Introducing the Model Context Protocol](https://www.anthropic.com/news/model-context-protocol)
  MCP's original announcement, straight from its creator. **Use for**: the canonical definition, before cross-checking current governance status.
- [Model Context Protocol — Agentic AI Foundation announcement](https://blog.modelcontextprotocol.io/posts/2025-12-09-mcp-joins-agentic-ai-foundation/)
  MCP's move to Linux Foundation governance (Dec 2025), co-founded by Anthropic, Block, and OpenAI. **Use for**: confirming MCP is now a cross-vendor standard, not an Anthropic-only protocol.
- [mastra.ai](https://mastra.ai/) / [Mastra — About](https://mastra.ai/about)
  Primary source for the framework itself: what it does, who built it, funding. **Use for**: ground truth on Mastra before trusting secondary write-ups.
- [LangChain — AI agent frameworks guide](https://www.langchain.com/resources/ai-agent-frameworks)
  A framework vendor's own map of the competitive landscape (read with the appropriate grain of salt). **Use for**: a snapshot of how the field is consolidating, from a well-informed but non-neutral source.
- [Palantir — Ontology system docs](https://www.palantir.com/docs/foundry/architecture-center/ontology-system)
  Primary source for the enterprise/agent sense of "ontology" that's reshaping the term's meaning in 2025–2026 discourse. **Use for**: distinguishing this usage from the classical knowledge-representation sense.
- [Hugging Face blog](https://huggingface.co/blog)
  Primary source for what's actually shipping on the platform (Xet storage, Inference Providers, robotics push). **Use for**: checking Hugging Face's current role before relying on older "it's just a model zoo" descriptions.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/LocalLLaMA](https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/)
  Grounded, hands-on discussion among practitioners running open-weight models themselves. **Use for**: a sentiment/rumor-mill supplement, lower trust than primary sources but useful for "is this actually good" reality checks.
- [Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/) (podcast/newsletter, swyx & Alessio)
  Practitioner-facing, technical-but-accessible. **Use for**: tracking what builders are actually shipping and which tools matter day to day.
- [Interconnects](https://www.interconnects.ai/) (Nathan Lambert)
  The strongest technical newsletter specifically on post-training methods and open-weight models. **Use for**: plain-English explanations of reasoning-model mechanics.
